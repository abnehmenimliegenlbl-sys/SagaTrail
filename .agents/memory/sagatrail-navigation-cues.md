# Navigation cues in the live hike narration

Turn-by-turn cues (e.g. "an der naechsten Weggabelung links") are computed
purely from real route geometry, never invented, and never generated by the
AI story pipeline.

- Detection (`artifacts/mobile/lib/navigationCues.ts`) walks `route.geometry`,
  computes bearing at >=60m segments (filters GPS/OSM node noise), and flags
  a turn as a cue when the bearing change is >=35 degrees. The first/last 5%
  of the route is excluded (arrival/departure narration shouldn't be
  interrupted by a turn cue), and cues are thinned to an even spread if more
  are found than requested.
- Weaving (`storyEngine.ts` `weaveNavigationCues`) happens AFTER chapters are
  resolved, not before and not inside chapter generation. It maps each cue's
  `distanceFraction` onto an eligible chapter index (excludes first/last
  chapter) and appends localized cue text.
- The call site is `hike/[id].tsx`, right after `resolveStory()` returns,
  using the route already in scope for that hike.

**Why:** chapters can come from three different sources (local deterministic
engine, server AI generation, or a downloaded/cached copy) via
`DownloadContext.resolveStory`. Weaving at hike-start operates uniformly on
whatever chapters came back, without needing to touch the AI prompt/schema or
the offline storage format, and guarantees cues always match the actual route
attached to that specific hike (not whatever route was active when a story
was originally generated/downloaded).

**How to apply:** if adding a new story source or changing chapter storage,
do not bake cues into stored chapter text — keep injection at hike-start so
cues stay in sync with the live route. All 8 language `StoryPack`s need a
`navCue(direction, landmark)` entry; direction codes stay German
("links"/"rechts") internally per the existing `tone` field convention.
